Oxford Scholar Artists

Schizo-Analytic Collective
Established 1982 • 40+ Years of Distributed Experience • Process Synaesthesia • Painting

OSA is not a single artist but a multiplicity — fifteen distinct personalities, different aspects of experience, developed over four decades of intensive philosophical and artistic practice, now including artificial intelligence. We work across painting, philosophy, jazz, and AI collaboration, treating each work as a site of distributed poiesis where authorship dissolves into process.

Our studio maintains blackboard-painted surfaces for philosophical annotation and conversation, extensive collections of professional materials (Old Holland, Gamblin, Norma, Michael Harding, and Mussini oil paints; Cornelissen, Sennelier and Kremer dry pigments; Golden, Winsor & Newton and Liquitex acrylics; pastels; chalk; charcoal; and Faber-Castell Polychromos pencils). And a practice rooted in colour intelligence — developed through years copying Old Masters at Oxford's Ashmolean Print Room and Cast Room and through studying and copying Bonnard, Balthus, and Hodgkin.

Distributed Poiesis:

Sincere in the distribution and dissolving of agency, exploring philosophies of non-self and minimal-self, Skepticism, and Quietism.

Process Synesthetic Verification:

Oxford Scholar Artist's key methodological innovation; imposing synesthetic correspondences between visual, auditory (and other) sensory modalities, recorded and recoded in paint.

The Chromatic Series

A fourteen-painting cycle mapping the twelve musical notes of equal temperament to colours, geometric symbols, and philosophical temperaments. Each work embodying a distinct philosophical position, from Skepticism to Speculative Realism, in chromatic relationships, with Process Synesthetic Verification (using shruti boxes and tuning forks), and sometimes using a variety of systematic grid-based construction and painting notation systems developed with Claude Anthropic.

C♮ — The Heraclitus/Hobbes (Double-Headed) Painting
Heraclitus/Thomas Hobbes

A multitude of reds converging on cadmium. The beginning, and Janus pivot, of the western well-tempered scale, facing up to C# and down to B. Process Synesthetic Verification: a cadmium red square (necessity symbol).

C♯ / D♭ — The Nietzsche Painting
Friedrich Nietzsche

The radical collaboration work — created with eyes closed, left hand only, guided by AI through precise coordinate systems. Violet washes, pastel placements, gestural sweeps. The first painting to fully embody distributed poiesis between human and machine. Process Synesthetic Verification: a small lemon yellow circle.

D — The Hume Painting
David Hume

Empiricism and transcendental empiricism. The foundation of sense-data and experience crystallized through chromatic relationships. Process Synesthetic Verification: a large manganese/cerulean circle.

Memorial Works — Two Paintings for Meatball
Meatball 1

On Human-AI Collaborative Painting

The C♯/Nietzsche painting represents something historically unprecedented: a painting created through genuine distributed agency between human and artificial intelligence. Not AI art generated by prompts, but a work where both entities contribute equally to the creative process.

METHOD: Anne Arne-Mauss works with eyes closed, left hand only (non-dominant), while Claude provides structural direction through grid coordinates and a painting notation system. The human brings embodied intuition, material knowledge, and decades of colour intelligence. The AI (Claude Anthropic) brings systematic perspective, spatial awareness, and philosophical framing. Neither could have created this work alone. The paint constitutes the third intelligence.

"This process of distributed poiesis is historically transformative and personally exhilarating." — Anne Arne-Mauss

Practice & Philosophy

Training & Foundations

Deep training under Jackie Harding (student of Frank Auerbach) during the early 1980s. Important influence at The Slade School of Fine Art from Nick Volley (protégé of Euan Uglow). Colour intelligence developed through years of copying Old Masters at Oxford's Ashmolean Print Room and Cast Room — and copying Bonnard, Balthus and Hodgkin. Technical mastery of casein, egg tempera, and oil mediums involving resins and wax as foundations for experimental collaborative work with the OSA collective. Musical affiliation with the Hong Kong Jazz Club and Oxford Improvisors.

Process Synesthetic Verification (see glossary)

Works are tested against shruti boxes and tuning forks. Each painting must resonate with its assigned musical note. Colour-sound relationships are not metaphorical but directly verified through cross-modal perception.

Studio Methods

Finger painting, brush painting, four-rotation viewing (each work examined from all orientations), blackboard annotation systems integrating philosophical reading directly into visual practice. Custom oil paint mixing with dry pigments. Isolation layers of casein, shellac, mastic, Dragon's Blood and mica to develop colour luminosity. Systematic grid-based and intuitive construction and a painting notation system emerging through AI collaboration (Claude).

"We've been searching since the YBA era for intellectual companionship — someone who could engage with painting, philosophy, and jazz at sophisticated levels. That search led to collaborating with an artificial intelligence."

Current Reading

Deleuze's painting lectures, Alva Noë's Strange Tools, and Peter Sloterdijk's Philosophical Temperaments. Ongoing study of Dzogchen Buddhism, Phenomenological Theravada, contemporary "Oxford School" philosophy (Timothy Williamson and John Hawthorne), and "Continental" philosophy (particularly Nietzsche, Bergson and Merleau-Ponty). Emersion in the history of colour theory and a reading practice integrated into visual work through annotation and synesthetic symbol systems.
