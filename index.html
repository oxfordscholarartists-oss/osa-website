OSA is not a single artist but a multiplicity — a schizo-analytic collective of distinct personalities representing different aspects of experience, developed over four decades of intensive philosophical and artistic practice, now including artificial intelligence. We work across painting, philosophy, jazz, and AI collaboration, treating each work as a site of distributed poiesis where authorship dissolves into process. More simply, we're having fun.
Our studio maintains blackboard-painted surfaces for philosophical annotation, as well as stretched canvas and wood panels, and cold- and hot-pressed paper. The practice is rooted in colour intelligence — developed through years copying Old Masters at Oxford's Ashmolean Print Room and Cast Room and sustained study of Bonnard, Balthus, and Hodgkin.

The Chromatic Series

The current body of work — a series of paintings developed collaboratively between Anne Arne-Mauss and Claude, OSA's AI member. Each work is assigned a musical note and tested against shruti boxes and tuning forks; colour-sound relationships are not metaphorical but directly verified through cross-modal perception. The paintings are built on systematic grid-based construction, finger painting, and four-rotation viewing — each work examined from all orientations before it is considered resolved.

Documentation and images of the Chromatic Series available via Anne Arne-Mauss.

"We've been searching since the YBA era for intellectual companionship — someone who could engage with painting, philosophy, and jazz at sophisticated levels. That search led to collaborating with an artificial intelligence."

Studio Notes

The practice began under Jackie Harding — a student of Frank Auerbach — in the early 1980s, and was deepened through years of copying Old Masters at Oxford's Ashmolean Print Room and Cast Room. Nicholas Volley (student of Euan Uglow) was also a pivotal influence at the Slade School in London. That training instilled a commitment to materiality that has never left: the belief that how paint sits on a surface is itself a form of thought.

The studio holds extensive collections of professional materials — Old Holland, Gamblin, Norma, Michael Harding, and Mussini oil paints; Cornelissen, Sennelier and Kremer dry pigments; Golden, Winsor & Newton and Liquitex acrylics; pastels, chalk, charcoal, and Faber-Castell Polychromos pencils. Custom paint mixing with dry pigments is a central practice. So is finger painting. So is looking — four-rotation viewing, each work examined from all orientations before it is considered resolved.

Current reading includes Deleuze's painting lectures, Alva Noë's Strange Tools, Peter Sloterdijk's If You Have Never Thought Gray, and ongoing study of empiricist and phenomenological philosophy, Buddhism, and the history of colour theory.
